The Wire Words

The wire words is an electrical wiring competition which tests the understanding and wiring capabilities of the participants. The competition is designed in three levels to meet the event objectives.

Objectives :

To test the basic knowledge about electrical wiring.

To test the hands on performance.

To test the practical knowledge.


Level 1 - 1 Hr

The participants will be given a set of questions which they will have to answer in the given time. Based on the result the initial screening will be done. The question pattern can be multiple choice or numerical, but mainly based on electrical wiring.

Also the participants will be given a set of conditions and layout and they will have to develop the circuit that satisfies it.

Level 2 - 1 Hr

The participants will have to wire up the circuit they have developed in the previous level within the specified time. The circuits which are completed within the time limit will be considered for evaluation and based on the result the participants will qualify to the next level.

Level 3 - 2 Hr

The participants will be given a civil plan. They will have to draw the wiring diagram for it and do the estimation within the time limit. On completion of the time limit their performance will be evaluated and the winners will be decided.


A team of 2 members can participate in this event.

First Level : Questionnaire

It consists of minimum 20 questions. 15 questions based on wiring and components. 5 questions general. Scores are to be the criteria for selection to the next level. In case a tie occurs the team with highest score in 'wiring and component' specific question section should be given priority. The circuit diagram for the provided layout should be accurate.

Second level : Hands on

Only the final circuit will be evaluated. The accuracy of the output will be the first criteria. In case of a tie the time taken for implementation along with neatness of the circuit will be considered.

Third level : Being pratical

Hand drawing of the wiring diagram is needed. The component specifications with quantity are also necessary. Evaluation will be based on the practicality, neatness, closeness to standards, knowledge of component ratings etc.
